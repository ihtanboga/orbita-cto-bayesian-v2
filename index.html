The Model

One Equation, Five Trials

Every trial's symptom result is decomposed into a real PCI effect, a CTO-specific modifier, and noise. Two binary switches (CTO and sham) turn terms on and off.

Daily Transition OR = μ + δopen × CTO + ε
TermWhat it capturesSource
μTrue PCI benefit over sham (daily PO scale)ORBITA-2 Bayesian PO: β=0.199, SE=0.029
δopenCTO collateral attenuation + open-label placebo inflation (inseparable)EuroCTO, COMET-CTO, DECISION-CTO
εBetween-trial heterogeneityτ ~ HalfNormal(0.10)
Why δopen instead of separate δCTO + bias? All CTO trials are open-label. The collateral attenuation and placebo inflation are always active together — inseparable with current data. We report their sum honestly and show bias scenarios below.

Cutting Feedback

μ is locked to ORBITA-2's posterior. CTO trial data can only update δopen and τ, not the base PCI effect. This prevents noisy open-label data from corrupting the precise sham-controlled anchor.

Uncertainty-Aware Crosswalk

CTO trials reported SAQ angina frequency (0–100 scale). ORBITA-CTO uses a daily ordinal diary. We bridge them using ORBITA-2 (which measured both), with the conversion uncertainty propagated through the model.

Bottom Line

ORBITA-CTO will almost certainly fix the physiology. Whether the patient notices — that is the question this trial begins to answer.

Predicted daily transition OR: 1.05  |  ~1 extra angina-free day over 6 months  |  ~44% power at N=50

This is a pilot. It proves feasibility, not efficacy. A powered confirmatory trial would need N>300.

Simulation, not prediction. Model is assumption-driven, not identified. Sensitivity range: OR 0.98–1.08 across 10 scenarios.
